# Choosing Glass for Odisha Climate

Odisha homes face **high humidity**, **strong monsoon rains**, **intense summer heat**, and in coastal areas like Puri **salt air**. Glass choice should match room orientation and how you use the space.

## Quick selection guide

| Situation | Recommended glass |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Shaded internal room, budget priority | Single glazed (minimum) |
| Most bedrooms and living rooms | Double glazed |
| West-facing or top-floor sun exposure | Double glazed with low-E, or triple |
| Main road or market noise | Double glazed with laminated pane |
| Ground-floor street exposure, security concern | Laminated safety glass |
| Puri / coastal sea-facing | Double or triple with corrosion-aware hardware |

## Coastal homes (Puri, Konark, coastal Cuttack)

Salt mist accelerates corrosion on exposed hardware. We specify marine-suitable fittings where needed and recommend regular rinsing of tracks after sea-facing storms. Glass itself is sealed within the frame — profile and hardware maintenance matters most.

## Monsoon considerations

Glass type does not replace good **installation and drainage**. Even the best IGU will fail if water pools in tracks. Ensure your installer follows LIPU monsoon sealing procedures documented in our installation guides.

## Do not over-specify

Triple glazing everywhere adds cost and weight. Use it where heat or noise genuinely warrants it — typically one or two critical rooms, not every small bathroom ventilator.
